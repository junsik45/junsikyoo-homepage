I am a computational physicist working at the intersection of **lattice QCD**, **high-performance computing**, and **machine learning**.

My research develops first-principles numerical methods, scalable scientific software, and interpretable AI tools for understanding high-dimensional computational systems. I have worked on precision Standard Model and beyond-the-Standard-Model calculations using lattice QCD, including proton decay matrix elements, electroweak radiative corrections, nucleon structure, and CP-violating operators.

A common theme across my work is extracting reliable structure from large, noisy, high-dimensional computations. This includes lattice field theory calculations on leadership-class supercomputers, variance-reduction methods for Monte Carlo simulations, and machine-learning diagnostics for neural representation geometry.
